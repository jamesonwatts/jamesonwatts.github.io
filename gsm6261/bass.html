Innovators and Imitators

The basic idea - there are two types of customers

Innovators:

  • they buy the product using the information about the product, advertisement, etc.

Imitators:

  • their decision is based on experience of other people, their ratings, etc.

Model Parameters

  • m = size of market
  • p = coefficient of innovation
  • q = coefficient of imitation
  • N = cumulative adoptions so far
  • n = adoptions predicted in current period

Liklihood of a new adoption at time \(t\) is,

\[p+q\frac{N}{m}\]

We multiply this by the remainder of the market,

\[m-N\]

in order to get current year adoptions,

\[ n=[p+q\frac{N}{m}][m-N] \]

Model Estimation

When you multiply these terms out you get \[ n=pm+(q-p)N-\frac{q}{m}N^2 \]

which looks a lot like a problem we can estimate using OLS where, \[ n=a+bN+cN^2. \] Since we know that market size occurs when \(n=0\), we can solve for m using the quadradic equation, and the other variables follow. \[ m = \frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2c} \] \[ p = a/m \\ q=-mc \]

Solution

Or we can solve the parameter-free verion via the implied differential equation

\[ \frac{dN(t)}{dt}=pm+(q-p)N(t-1)-\frac{q}{m}N(t-1)^2 \] which is: \[ N(t)=m\frac{1-e^{-(p+q)t}}{1+(\frac{q}{p})e^{-(p+q)t}} \] and the corresponding function \[ N'(t)=n(t)=m\frac{(p+q)^2e^{-(p+q)t}}{p[1+\frac{q}{p}e^{-(p+q)t}]^2} \]

Graphs

Given that m is just a constant, we can view graphs of the probability mass cumulative density using the following function

graphs <- function(p,q,t.max) {
  t <-seq(from=0,to=t.max,by=0.01) 
  Bass.f <- ((p+q)^2/p)*exp(-(p+q)*t)/(1+(q/p)*exp(-(p+q)*t))^2
  Bass.F <- (1-exp(-(p+q)*t))/(1+(q/p)*exp(-(p+q)*t))
  par(mfrow=c(1,2))
  plot(t, Bass.f, type="l")
  plot(t, Bass.F, type="l")
}

Values of P and Q

remember Rogers five factors?

  • Relative advantage: the degree to which a product is better than the product it replaces
  • Compatibility: the degree to which a product is consistent with existing values and experiences
  • Complexity: the degree to which a product is difficult to understand and use
  • Trialability: the degree to which a product may be experimented with on a limited basis
  • Observability: the degree to which product usage and impact are visible to others
